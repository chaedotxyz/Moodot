## FAQ

**Where is my data stored?**
All of your notes and mood entries are stored locally on your Mac. Moodot does not sync or upload your data to any server or cloud service. Your journal is private and stays on your device.

**What happens to my data if I delete Moodot?**
Uninstalling Moodot will permanently remove all app data from your device, including your notes, mood history, and analytics. This action cannot be undone. If you wish to preserve your entries, please back them up before uninstalling.

**How does saving work in Moodot?**
Moodot automatically saves your note to your device within 3 seconds of writing. There is no need to manually save — simply write your entry, set your mood, and your note will be saved and timestamped shortly after you stop typing.

**Does Moodot require an internet connection?**
No. Moodot is fully offline and does not require an internet connection for any of its features, including writing notes, tracking moods, and viewing Mood Analytics.

**How does the mood spectrum work?**
Each note you create can be tagged with one of eight mood states: melancholy, sad, calm, neutral, energized, anxious, intense, or euphoric. The color of each mood is reflected in the sidebar and throughout the app so you can feel the pattern at a glance.

**How does Mood Analytics work?**
The Mood Analytics view shows a count of how many notes you've written for each mood state, sorted from most to least frequent. Each mood card displays the total count and its percentage out of all your notes.

**How do I recover a deleted note?**
Deleted notes are sent to the Trash. You can restore any note from the Trash at any time. Notes that are permanently deleted from the Trash cannot be recovered.

**The app is not opening or behaving unexpectedly — what should I do?**
Please try quitting the app fully and relaunching it. If the issue persists, restart your Mac and open Moodot again. If you continue to experience problems, contact us at **chaedotxyz@gmail.com** with a description of the issue and your macOS version.
